A cleanroom is a lab for making micro/nanodevices that need very clean conditions. Micro/nanodevices are tiny devices that have many uses in different fields. To make them, a cleanroom uses three steps: deposition, lithography, and etching. In this report, we will explain the cleanroom and its machines and how they work.

The structure of the cleanroom

It is a specialized laboratory for fabricating micro/nanodevices that require a very low level of environmental contamination, such as dust, airborne particles, microbes, or chemical vapors. A cleanroom is designed to control the temperature, humidity, air pressure, and air flow within the laboratory. The cleanliness of a cleanroom is measured by the number of particles per cubic meter at a specified size range. The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) has defined nine classes of cleanrooms, from ISO 1 (the cleanest) to ISO 9 (the dirtiest). The typical ambient air in an urban area is equivalent to an ISO 9 cleanroom.
  • Air filters
    : These are devices that remove particles and contaminants from the air entering or circulating within the cleanroom. There are different types of air filters, such as high-efficiency particulate air (HEPA) filters, ultra-low penetration air (ULPA) filters, or activated carbon filters. Air filters are usually installed in the ceiling or walls of the cleanroom.
  • Air showers
    : These are chambers that blow high-speed jets of filtered air onto the personnel or materials entering or exiting the cleanroom. The purpose of air showers is to remove any loose particles or contaminants from the surfaces of the objects or people passing through them.
  • Laminar flow hoods
    : These are workstations that provide a localized area of clean air within the cleanroom. Laminar flow hoods have fans and filters that create a unidirectional and uniform flow of air over the work surface. Laminar flow hoods are used for handling sensitive materials or processes that require a high level of cleanliness.
  • Glove boxes
    : These are sealed containers that have gloves attached to them, allowing the manipulation of materials or devices inside them without direct contact with the external environment. Glove boxes are used for handling hazardous, toxic, or infectious substances that require isolation from the surrounding air.
  • Cleanroom garments
    : These are special clothing and accessories that are worn by the personnel working in the cleanroom. Cleanroom garments include suits, gloves, masks, hats, shoes, and goggles. The purpose of cleanroom garments is to prevent the shedding of skin cells, hair, fibers, or other particles that could contaminate the cleanroom environment.

The main machines

Deposition machines: These machines are used to create thin layers of materials on a substrate by various methods, such as sputtering, evaporation, chemical vapor deposition (CVD), or atomic layer deposition (ALD). Sputtering machines use a plasma to bombard a target material and eject atoms that are deposited on the substrate. Evaporation machines use a high vacuum to heat a source material and vaporize it onto the substrate. CVD machines use a gas mixture to react with the substrate and form a solid film. ALD machines use sequential pulses of precursor gases to deposit one atomic layer at a time.
Lithography machines: These machines are used to transfer a pattern onto a substrate by using light, electrons, or ions. Electron beam lithography machines use a focused beam of electrons to write the pattern directly on a resist-coated substrate. Ion beam lithography machines use a focused beam of ions to do the same. Optical lithography machines use a mask and a light source to project the pattern onto the substrate. Nanoimprint lithography machines use a mold and a pressure system to imprint the pattern onto the substrate.
Etching machines: are devices that are used to create patterns or shapes on the surface of a material by removing unwanted portions of it. There are two main types of etching machines: chemical and physical. Chemical etching machines use a chemical agent, such as a liquid or a gas, to react with the material and dissolve the parts that are not protected by a mask. Physical etching machines use a physical force, such as a plasma or an ion beam, to bombard the material and sputter away the parts that are not shielded by a mask.

A plasma etching machine is a device that uses a plasma (ionized gas) to create patterns on a material by removing unwanted parts. It works by applying an electric field to a gas and creating a plasma inside a vacuum chamber. The plasma contains charged particles that hit the material and either knock off atoms (physical etching) or react with them and form volatile compounds (chemical etching). The plasma etching machine can control the ratio of physical and chemical etching by changing the electric field and the gas composition. It can also use a mask to protect some areas of the material from being etched.

Plasma etching machines are used in various fields, such as electronics, microfabrication, art, and jewelry. They can create complex and detailed patterns on different materials and improve their properties. Plasma etching machines have some advantages over wet etching machines, such as higher resolution, lower damage, and less waste. However, they also have some disadvantages, such as higher cost, lower throughput, and higher energy consumption.
